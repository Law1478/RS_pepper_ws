Now we arrive at a display of chaotic kinematics. This is a double pendulum, otherwise known as a "chaotic pendulum". 
This is a demonstration of the incredible complexities which kinematics can contribute to. The double pendulum experiment is one of an array of examples which depict a great change in output from a relatively negligible change in starting variables. Other examples you might be familiar with include turbulence, the butterfly effect, and the 3 body problem.
The discovery Lorenz systems gave way to what is called "Chaos Theory". This theory stated that things which you once thought were completely random were predictable if all variables were accounted for with even more precision than you could produce or even measure.
